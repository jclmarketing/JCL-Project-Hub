Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about domestic electrical work answered by our team.

How do I know if my house needs a re-wire?

Signs that your home may need a re-wire include: old round-pin sockets, a dated fuse box with rewirable fuses, rubber or fabric-insulated cables, frequent blown fuses or tripping, scorch marks around sockets or switches, and flickering lights. If your property has not been re-wired in the last 25 to 30 years, we would recommend an Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) to assess the condition of your wiring. We offer free assessments and can advise on the best course of action for your home.

Do I need to move out during a re-wire?

In most cases, you do not need to move out during a re-wire. We work room by room, ensuring you always have power and essential services available. We plan the work carefully to minimise disruption and will always discuss the schedule with you beforehand. For full re-wires of larger properties, there may be brief periods where the power needs to be switched off, but we will always give you advance notice.

What is a consumer unit upgrade and do I need one?

A consumer unit (or fuse board) upgrade replaces your old fuse box with a modern unit featuring RCD protection, which can detect faults and cut the power in milliseconds to prevent electric shocks and fires. Current regulations require all new consumer units to be metal-clad with surge protection. If your home still has an old-style fuse board with rewirable fuses or an older MCB board without RCD protection, an upgrade is highly recommended for your safety.

Can you install an EV charger at my home?

Yes, we are fully qualified to install electric vehicle charging points at your home. We can install all major brands of EV charger and will assess your existing electrical supply to ensure it can support the additional load. A dedicated circuit is run from your consumer unit to the charger location, and we handle all aspects of the installation including any necessary upgrades to your supply. We can also advise on available government grants to help with the cost.

Do you provide certificates for domestic electrical work?

Yes, all notifiable domestic electrical work is fully certified and registered with the relevant building control body. Depending on the type of work, you will receive either an Electrical Installation Certificate (EIC) or a Minor Electrical Installation Works Certificate (MEIWC). These certificates are essential for your records, home insurance and any future property sale. We also provide test results and a condition report upon completion of the work.
