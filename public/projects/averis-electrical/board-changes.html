Consumer Unit & Board Changes

Upgrading old fuse boxes to modern consumer units with RCD protection. Essential for safety, insurance, and compliance with current regulations.

Why Upgrade Your Consumer Unit?

Your consumer unit (fuse box) is the heart of your property's electrical system. It distributes power to every circuit and, crucially, provides protection against electrical faults. If your property still has an old-style fuse box with rewirable fuses, it is likely lacking the modern safety features that protect against electric shock and electrical fires.

A modern consumer unit fitted with RCDs (Residual Current Devices) or RCBOs (Residual Current Breaker with Overcurrent protection) can detect faults and disconnect the power in milliseconds, significantly reducing the risk of injury or fire. Upgrading your board is one of the most important electrical safety improvements you can make to any property.

BS 7671 18th Edition Compliant

All of our consumer unit installations are carried out in full compliance with the current BS 7671 18th Edition Wiring Regulations, including the latest Amendment 2 requirements. This means your new board will include surge protection devices (SPDs) where required, metal consumer unit enclosures for fire safety, and full RCD or RCBO protection across all circuits.

Safety, Insurance & Compliance

Many insurance providers now require properties to have a modern consumer unit with RCD protection. If you are a landlord, having an up-to-date consumer unit is also essential for meeting your legal obligations. Upgrading your board ensures your property is safe, compliant, and properly protected.

Every board change we carry out comes with a full Electrical Installation Certificate (EIC), and we notify Building Control on your behalf as part of our NICEIC-approved contractor status. This gives you complete peace of mind and proper documentation for your records.
